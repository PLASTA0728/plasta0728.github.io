ESPR Portrait

This piece was created as part of my “quest” challenge on the final day of ESPR '24. During the program, I reconnected with my passion for art—a passion that had quieted over time due to my focus on STEM and my hesitation about sharing my work. ESPR reignited my love for creating, bringing back an excitement I hadn't felt in a long time.

As part of my challenge, I decided to make individual portraits for everyone in the camp. I designed the backgrounds with ESPR's theme colors, blue and yellow, using Python code to vary the brightness for each piece. The shifting backgrounds added an extra layer of difficulty in choosing the right skin tones, pushing my skills further in color selection. (Fun fact: while the quest was meant to be completed within 24 hours, it ended up taking me a bit longer—though in the time-lapse replay, it amusingly condensed to exactly 24 hours, 23 minutes, and 12 seconds!)
